PRIVACY POLICY

Last updated: April 6, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Lighthouse (the "Organization", "we", "our", or "us") collects, uses, stores, and protects personal information when you use our website, donation tools, volunteer forms, and related services.

1) INFORMATION WE COLLECT 

We may collect:
- Contact information (name, email, phone number, address)
- Donation and payment-related records (amount, date, method, campaign)
- Volunteer and supporter profile information
- Communication preferences and outreach history
- Technical usage data (IP address, browser type, pages visited, timestamps)

2) HOW WE USE INFORMATION

We use personal information to:
- Process donations and provide receipts
- Communicate updates, impact reports, and campaign information
- Manage volunteer and supporter relationships
- Improve our website, programs, and outreach operations
- Comply with legal and financial reporting requirements

3) LEGAL BASIS (WHERE APPLICABLE)

Depending on jurisdiction, we process data under one or more of these bases:
- Consent
- Performance of a contract or requested service
- Compliance with legal obligations
- Legitimate organizational interests that do not override individual rights

4) SHARING OF INFORMATION

We do not sell personal information. We may share data with:
- Trusted service providers (e.g., payment processors, email platforms, hosting providers)
- Professional advisors (legal, accounting, audit) when required
- Authorities when required by law or to protect rights and safety

All third-party processors are expected to handle data securely and only for authorized purposes.

5) DATA RETENTION

We retain personal data only as long as needed for:
- Donation and supporter management
- Legal, accounting, tax, and audit obligations
- Legitimate program and operational needs

When no longer needed, data is deleted, anonymized, or securely archived as required.

6) DATA SECURITY

We implement reasonable technical and organizational safeguards, including:
- Access controls and role-based permissions
- Secure storage and encrypted transmission where appropriate
- Monitoring and incident response procedures

No system is 100% secure, but we continuously work to protect your data.

7) YOUR RIGHTS

Depending on your location, you may have rights to:
- Access personal data we hold about you
- Correct inaccurate information
- Request deletion of certain data
- Restrict or object to certain processing
- Withdraw consent (where processing is based on consent)

To exercise these rights, contact us using the details below.

8) COOKIES AND TRACKING

Our website may use cookies or similar technologies for:
- Basic site functionality
- Analytics and performance measurement
- Improving user experience

You can manage cookie preferences through browser settings.

9) INTERNATIONAL DATA TRANSFERS

If data is transferred across borders, we take reasonable steps to ensure appropriate safeguards consistent with applicable law.

10) CHILDREN'S PRIVACY

Our services are not intended for unsupervised use by children where consent is required by law. If you believe a child provided data improperly, contact us for review and removal.

11) CHANGES TO THIS POLICY

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Updated versions will include a revised "Last updated" date.
